    SafeMoon price: Cryptocurrency nears all-time high value after 9% price rise

    SAFEMOON price has continued to rise throughout the month, as the new cryptocurrency aims to compete with some of the most popular cryptos available, including Bitcoin, Ethereum and Dogecoin. Here's everything you need to know about SafeMoon - including whether you should buy the new currency.

    By Matt Atherton
    PUBLISHED: 19:32, Sat, May 8, 2021 | UPDATED: 07:19, Wed, May 12, 2021

    SafeMoon is one of the newest cryptocurrencies available, and has quickly burst to fame. The crypto continued its meteoric rise today, as it jumped up to nine percent in value in just 24 hours.

    SafeMoon was only created a few, short months ago, but it's already making its name known among the cryptocurrency elite.

    It's relatively unusual, compared with its predecessors, however.

    Investors are punished if they try to withdraw or trade their coins.

    The idea is to try and dissuade investors from cashing out, which would help to continue the price rise.

    READ MORE: SafeMoon price - new cryptocurrency jumps 45% in just one day

    SafeMoon price: New cryptocurrency nears all-time high after 9% price rise (Image: GETTY Images)

    Anybody that wants to sell their SafeMoon tokens faces a 10 percent charge.

    Five percent of that charge is redistributed to existing investors, to reward them for sticking around.

    SafeMoon has continued its meteoric rise over the past day.

    It jumped to $0.0000099 on Saturday afternoon - the equivalent to about a nine percent price rise.

    The crypto is edging closer to its all-time high, which it hit on April 20.

    SafeMoon's price reached $0.00001399, before dipping by about 50 percent just a few days later.

    Since then, it's been slowly rebuilding its momentum - particularly since May 4.

    If SafeMoon continues its current trajectory, it could break its all-time record over the next few days.

    Should you buy SafeMoon tokens?

    Investing in cryptocurrency is incredibly risky, and you'll need to really think it through before jumping in.

    It's highly volatile, and you'll need to be tracking its progress constantly.

    You should only buy SafeMoon tokens if you're absolutely sure that you can afford to lose the money.

    Investors have warned that SafeMoon is liable to 'pump and dump' schemes.

      If you do decide to buy SafeMoon tokens, you can do it on four different cryptocurrency exchanges.

      SafeMoon is available on PancakeSwap, BitMart, WhiteBit, and Bakery Swap.

      But it's a relatively convoluted process, compared with other cryptocurrencies.

      You'll need to buy Binance coins first, and then go on to purchase SafeMoon.
