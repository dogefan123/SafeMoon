              • cost basis for static reflections

                By Thomas Weicker · Posted 1 hour ago

                Checking how much SafeMoon you get in reflections is easy IF you are able to search for your wallet on BSCscan, go to bscscan.com and search for your wallet address, it's under holders, then you can see a list of your transactions.  In that list of transactions you should be able to see precisely how many SafeMoon you purchased, add up all of the SafeMoon that you purchased and then subtract that from how much SafeMoon you currently have and that will tell you how much SafeMoon you have received in reflections in total.  Now when it comes to taxing reflections, you people are making this more complicated than it needs to be lol.  First of all, I don't even know if SafeMoon reflections are even taxed, if it doesn't say anything about it in the tax code then it shouldn't be taxed, simple as that.  But IF SafeMoon reflections are taxed then it would be taxed at the time of selling it, it would be based on how many USD you got when you sold the reflections.  SafeMoon reflections might be considered income and therefore might be subject to your regular income taxes.  One thing I know is that SafeMoon reflections ARE NOT capital gains and therefore ARE NOT subject to capital gains taxes.  Capital gains taxes are based on what stocks and/or cryptocurrencies you BOUGHT and how much they went up in value from the time that you bought them to the time that you sold them, but you didn't BUY the SafeMoon reflections so they are NOT capital gains.  I'll give you an example.  Betty bought 100,000,000 SafeMoon, she will have to pay capital gains taxes if and only when she sells some or all of that 100,000,000 SafeMoon, but if she NEVER sells any of her 100,000,000 SafeMoon that she bought and she decides to hold onto it forever then she will NEVER have to pay capital gains taxes on it.  But Betty is receiving extra SafeMoon in the form of reflections, she does NOT have to pay capital gains taxes on those reflections, maybe those reflections could be subject to income taxes, I don't know.  But Betty's reflections are only extra SafeMoon that is more than the 100,000,000 SafeMoon that she bought, so as long as Betty only sells her reflections and never dips below 100,000,000 SafeMoon then she will never have to pay capital gains taxes.  IF Betty has to pay income taxes on those reflections then it would simply just be based on how many USD she got for selling the reflections AT THE TIME of selling, it is NOT based on the USD value at the time that she received the reflections, it is ONLY based on the USD value of the reflections at the time of selling those reflections.  I hope that helps, if anybody knows about if and how SafeMoon reflections are taxed then please talk about it here, we would like to know, thank you.
